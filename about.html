About Us

Our Story

How a message in a three-person group chat became a network of volunteers, a structured foundation, and a sea of lanterns.

01 The Discovery 02 First Visits 03 The Movement Grows 04 Building the Platform
Phase 01  ·  The Discovery

A message that was impossible to ignore.

Our story began with a message in a three-person group chat: "50 undocumented immigrant babies hidden in the mountains like refugees."

Reading further, what we discovered was difficult to ignore. Across Taiwan, children born to undocumented migrant workers were growing up without legal identity, healthcare access, or stable education. Their lives were shaped not by a single crisis, but by prolonged uncertainty. That realization unsettled us.

We didn't know what to do. But we knew we couldn't simply move on.

Phase 02  ·  The First Visits

Music became our first bridge.

We began visiting a nearby children's home mentioned in the article. What started as one visit gradually became consistent involvement. We read with the children, played music, and simply stayed.

"A guitar. A shared rhythm. A moment of laughter."

In those early days, we discovered something powerful — music brought light back into the room. It dissolved barriers that words couldn't. Music became our first bridge.

Phase 03  ·  The Movement Grows

A small gathering became something bigger.

We organized our first student-led fundraising concert. What began as a small gathering of friends grew into a structured volunteer system. More students joined — not because they had experience, but because they cared.

We also understood something deeper. These children lacked stable medical care. Without nationality, they were excluded from Taiwan's national health insurance system: an illness could become financially devastating for a family already living without legal protection.

Phase 04  ·  Building the Platform

So we chose to build one.

Many students wanted to help, but felt they were "just students" — lacking the guidance, structure, and platform to turn intention into action.

The Lantern Foundation was created not only to support children, but so that students can step into responsibility and transform concern into commitment.

Today, a network of volunteers across schools continues to stand alongside children through care and advocacy — while building a system where young people learn to lead through action.
